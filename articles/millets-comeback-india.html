Millets Make a Comeback: India’s Ancient Grains for the Future

Once sidelined, millets are returning as climate-resilient superfoods. Here’s why India is leading their revival.

Dr. Sunita Patel • Agricultural Scientist & Writer

Millets — including foxtail, finger, pearl, and little millet — were once staple crops across India. They require very little water, grow in poor soil, and are highly nutritious. Yet for decades they were overshadowed by rice and wheat.

Today, driven by climate change, nutrition awareness, and government initiatives like the International Year of Millets (2023), these ancient grains are experiencing a powerful revival.

Why Millets Matter Now

  • Climate resilience: They need 70% less water than rice.
  • Nutrition: High in fiber, protein, iron, and antioxidants.
  • Health benefits: Low glycemic index — excellent for diabetes management.
  • Sustainability: Support small farmers and biodiversity.

From traditional upma and rotis to modern millet burgers, smoothies, and snacks, chefs and home cooks are rediscovering their versatility.
