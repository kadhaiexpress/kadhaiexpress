The History of Paneer in Indian Cuisine

From ancient roots to the modern Indian kitchen — how paneer became one of India’s most beloved vegetarian proteins.

Paneer, the fresh Indian cheese, is one of the most versatile and beloved ingredients in vegetarian cooking. But where did it come from?

Ancient Origins

While the exact origin is debated, many food historians believe paneer-like cheeses were made in ancient India using natural acids like lemon juice or yogurt. References to fresh cheese appear in texts from the medieval period.

Influence of Persian and Afghan Cuisine

During the medieval period, Persian and Afghan influences brought new techniques. The word "paneer" itself comes from Persian, meaning "cheese". This period saw the popularization of paneer in rich gravies and kebabs.

Modern Popularity

Today, paneer is a staple across India — from North Indian butter masala to South Indian paneer 65. It is high in protein, easy to make at home, and incredibly versatile.
